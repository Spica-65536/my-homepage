About Me

Mathematics often makes learners feel “it’s difficult” or “I don’t understand,” and once someone gets stuck, it can be hard to recover. Through my research and development, I hope to help learners notice “I can solve this using what I already know,” and reduce their anxiety toward mathematics.

Outside my laboratory work, I teach as a private tutor (cram school instructor). I enjoy teaching and supporting others, and I also focus on mentoring junior members in my lab.

Research Overview

“Development of a Learning Support System to Facilitate Modularization of Knowledge in Geometric Proof Learning”

In learning geometric proofs, it is often difficult to decide which prior knowledge to apply and to construct a chain of reasoning. As a result, learners may rely on memorizing procedures, which can cause logical gaps. This research aims to develop a learning support system that models definitions and properties as modular “knowledge components,” maps them to each step of inference, visualizes the structure of reasoning, and provides feedback.
